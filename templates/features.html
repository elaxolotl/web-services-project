Managing Detained Goods

This API manages information about detained goods efficiently. Below is a brief explanation of each field in the schema to help understand its structure and functionality.

- id: A unique identifier for the detained good (read-only).
- name: The name of the detained good (required).
- container_id: The identifier of the container holding the good (required).
- opening_price: The initial price of the good when listed for auction (required).
- perishable: Indicates if the good is perishable (true/false) (required).
- expiry_date: The expiry date of the good, if applicable (optional).
- description: A detailed description of the detained good.
- guarantee: The amount required for bidders to enter the auction (refunded after the auction).
- manufacturer_details: Information about the manufacturer of the detained good.
- reason_for_detention: The reason why the good was detained.
- status: The status of the detained good (detained, on_sale, sold, or no_commercial_value).
- store: The storage location of the detained good.



Streamlined Auction Management with Automated Winner Assignment

Gavel API includes a daily task that automatically assigns auction winners, ensuring fairness and efficiency. Every 24 hours, the task checks for auctions that have reached their due date. If an auction has ended, the system automatically assigns the winner by selecting the highest bid. This feature eliminates manual intervention and ensures timely resolutions.

For example, when an auction is created with a due date 24 hours in the future, the task runs at the scheduled time, verifies the due date, and assigns the winner to the highest bidder. This process helps maintain fairness and keeps operations running smoothly.



Priority Handling of Perishable Goods

Our system ensures efficient management of detained goods by prioritizing perishable items during searches. When you search for detained goods, the system first displays perishable goods that are closest to their expiration date. This feature helps prevent spoilage and ensures timely processing.

Non-perishable goods are displayed next, ordered by the date they were added to the system. This logical prioritization helps customs officers efficiently handle goods based on urgency, reducing waste and improving operational flow.
